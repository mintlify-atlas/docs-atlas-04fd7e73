## Overview

Reviews are essential to the Kin Conecta community. They help tourists make informed decisions and help guides improve their services. This guide covers how to leave reviews as a tourist and how to manage and respond to reviews as a guide.

## Why Reviews Matter

### For the Community

- **Build trust** - Help other tourists make confident booking decisions
- **Recognize excellence** - Reward guides who provide exceptional experiences
- **Encourage improvement** - Provide constructive feedback
- **Maintain quality** - Keep the platform standard high

### For Tourists

- Reviews help you evaluate guides before booking
- Detailed feedback reveals what to expect
- Response patterns show guide professionalism
- Aggregate ratings indicate consistent quality

### For Guides

- Positive reviews attract more bookings
- Reviews improve search ranking
- Feedback helps refine your tours
- Responses show professionalism
- Reviews build your reputation

<Note>
  Both tourists and guides benefit from an active, honest review culture. Thoughtful reviews make the entire platform better.
</Note>

## For Tourists: Leaving Reviews

### When to Leave a Review

You can review a tour after it's completed:

- Review window opens immediately after tour end time
- You have 30 days to submit a review
- Cannot review tours you cancelled
- Must have attended the tour to review

### Accessing the Review Form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Reviews">
    Go to **Mis reseñas** in the sidebar or find the completed tour in **Mis viajes**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Tour to Review">
    Find the tour you want to review and click the review button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete Review Form">
    Fill out the rating and written review (detailed below).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit Review">
    Click submit. Your review is published immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Review Components

A complete review includes:

**Star Rating (Required)**
- 1 star - Poor experience
- 2 stars - Below expectations
- 3 stars - Acceptable/average
- 4 stars - Good experience
- 5 stars - Excellent experience

**Written Review (Required)**
- Minimum 50 characters
- Maximum 1000 characters
- Describe your experience
- Be specific and honest

**Category Ratings (Optional)**
Rate specific aspects:
- Guide knowledge
- Communication
- Value for money
- Organization

**Photos (Optional)**
- Upload photos from your tour
- Help others see what to expect
- Maximum 5 photos per review

### Writing Effective Reviews

**Be Specific**

Instead of: "Great tour!"

Write: "Luis was incredibly knowledgeable about the historic center. He took us to hidden courtyards I never would have found and shared fascinating stories about the architecture. The small group size made it easy to ask questions."

**Be Balanced**

Mention both positives and areas for improvement:

"The food tastings were delicious and authentic. María's passion for Oaxacan cuisine really showed. The only suggestion would be to provide a bit more time at each stop - we felt slightly rushed. Overall, highly recommend!"

**Be Constructive**

If something wasn't ideal, explain clearly:

Instead of: "Tour was disorganized."

Write: "The tour content was interesting, but we waited 15 minutes past start time for the guide to arrive. Clearer communication about delays would have been helpful."

**Include Details**

Help others understand what to expect:
- What you loved most
- Who the tour is best suited for
- Physical difficulty level
- Unique aspects of the experience
- Whether you'd recommend it

<Tip>
  The most helpful reviews are detailed, honest, and balanced. Future tourists and guides both benefit from thoughtful feedback.
</Tip>

### Review Examples

**Example: 5-Star Review**

```
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Amazing Street Food Experience

This was the highlight of our trip! Luis took us to six different 
vendors across Coyoacán and Roma neighborhoods. His knowledge of 
Mexican cuisine and relationships with local vendors made this so 
special. We tried everything from tamales to tlacoyos to fresh 
fruit with tajín. The group size (6 people) was perfect for getting 
attention and asking questions. 

Highly recommend for foodies and anyone wanting an authentic local 
experience. Wear comfortable shoes - we walked about 2 hours. Worth 
every peso!
```

**Example: 3-Star Review**

```
⭐⭐⭐

Good Tour with Some Issues

The guide was friendly and knowledgeable about Teotihuacán's history. 
We learned a lot about the pyramids. However, the group was much larger 
than advertised (20 people vs. 10 max listed), which made it hard to 
hear and ask questions. The pace was also quite fast - not much time 
for photos. 

Still a worthwhile experience if you want to learn about the site, but 
temper expectations about group size and pacing.
```

### What Not to Include

Avoid these in your reviews:

❌ **Personal attacks** - Focus on the experience, not the person
❌ **Unrelated complaints** - Keep feedback tour-specific
❌ **Threats or demands** - Handle disputes through support
❌ **Profanity** - Keep language appropriate
❌ **Discriminatory comments** - Never acceptable
❌ **Promotional content** - No ads or competitor mentions

<Note>
  Reviews violating community guidelines may be removed. Serious violations can result in account suspension.
</Note>

### Editing or Removing Reviews

After submitting a review:

- You can edit within 48 hours of posting
- Cannot delete reviews, but can contact support
- Guide responses remain even if you edit

- **Total reviews** - Number of reviews you've written

- **5 stars** - Percentage of 5-star reviews

## For Guides: Managing Reviews

### Viewing Your Reviews

Access **Tus reseñas** from the guide sidebar to see:

**Statistics Dashboard:**
- **Total de reseñas** - Total number of reviews received
- **Calificación promedio** - Your average star rating
- **5 estrellas** - Percentage of 5-star reviews
- **Este mes** - New reviews this month

**Reviews Feed:**
- Individual review cards
- Tourist name and profile photo
- Tour name reviewed
- Review date
- Star rating display
- Written review text
- Your response (if provided)

### Understanding Your Rating

Your overall rating is calculated from:

- All reviews across all your tours
- Weighted average of star ratings
- Updated immediately when new reviews come in
- Displayed prominently on your profile

**Rating Breakdown:**
- 4.8+ stars - Excellent (top-tier guide)
- 4.5-4.7 stars - Very good
- 4.0-4.4 stars - Good
- Below 4.0 - Needs improvement

<Tip>
  Most successful guides maintain ratings above 4.5 stars. This indicates consistent quality experiences.
</Tip>

## Responding to Reviews

Guides should respond to all reviews, especially detailed ones.

### Why Respond to Reviews

- Shows you value feedback
- Demonstrates professionalism
- Addresses concerns publicly
- Thanks tourists for their time
- Provides context to other readers
- Improves your reputation

### When to Respond

- Respond within 48 hours of receiving a review
- Prioritize negative reviews first
- Thank all reviewers eventually
- Keep responses current

### How to Respond

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Review">
    Go to **Tus reseñas** and find the review to respond to.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Response Button">
    Click **Responder a esta reseña** under the review.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write Your Response">
    Type your response following best practices (below).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit Response">
    Click submit. Your response appears immediately below the review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Response Best Practices

**For Positive Reviews:**

```
¡Gracias Sarah! I'm so happy you enjoyed the Historic Center tour. 
It was wonderful sharing the stories and architecture with you and 
your family. Hope to guide you again on your next visit to CDMX!

Saludos,
Luis
```

**For Constructive Feedback:**

```
Thank you for your feedback, María. I apologize for the delay at 
the start - that was due to unexpected traffic. I'm working on 
better communicating when delays occur. I appreciate you noting the 
tour content was strong. Your feedback helps me improve!

Best regards,
Carlos
```

**For Critical Reviews:**

```
I'm sorry your experience didn't meet expectations, James. The larger 
group size that day was due to a last-minute combination of two 
bookings. I should have communicated this change to you beforehand. 
I've since updated my policies to prevent this. Thank you for the 
constructive feedback about pacing - I'm incorporating more photo 
stops in future tours.
```

### Response Guidelines

**Do:**
- Thank the reviewer
- Address specific points raised
- Apologize for genuine issues
- Explain context when appropriate
- Stay professional and friendly
- Keep responses concise
- Personalize with their name

**Don't:**
- Get defensive or argumentative
- Make excuses for poor service
- Blame the tourist
- Write generic responses
- Ignore negative reviews
- Respond emotionally
- Violate tourist privacy

<Note>
  Future tourists read your responses carefully. Professional, thoughtful responses to criticism can actually increase bookings by showing you care about quality.
</Note>

### Handling Negative Reviews

Negative reviews are opportunities:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Don't React Immediately">
    Wait a few hours to respond calmly and professionally.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Acknowledge Concerns">
    Validate their experience even if you disagree with their interpretation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Take Responsibility">
    Own legitimate mistakes without making excuses.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Explain Context">
    Provide brief context if it helps clarify, but don't over-explain.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share Improvements">
    Mention how you're addressing the issue for future tours.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Offer to Discuss Offline">
    For serious issues, invite private conversation to resolve.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  A few negative reviews among many positive ones is normal and can actually increase trust - perfect ratings can seem fake. What matters is how you respond.
</Tip>

## Improving Your Ratings

### Deliver Excellent Experiences

The foundation of good reviews:

- Be knowledgeable and prepared
- Communicate clearly before and during tours
- Show enthusiasm and passion
- Respect tourist preferences and pace
- Handle issues professionally
- Go above and beyond when possible

### Set Proper Expectations

- Write accurate tour descriptions
- Be honest about difficulty levels
- Show real photos of the experience
- Communicate clearly about inclusions
- Confirm details before tours

### Ask for Reviews

At the end of great tours:

- Thank tourists for joining
- Mention you'd appreciate a review
- Don't pressure or incentivize
- Make it easy (send reminder message)

**Example follow-up message:**

```
Thanks again for joining today's tour! If you enjoyed the experience, 
I'd be grateful for a review on Kin Conecta. Your feedback helps me 
improve and helps other travelers discover the tour. Safe travels!
```

### Learn from Feedback

Use reviews to improve:

- Look for patterns in feedback
- Address recurring issues
- Double down on what people love
- Update tour descriptions based on comments
- Adjust pacing or content as needed

## Review Policies

### Platform Guidelines

Kin Conecta reviews must:

- Be based on actual experiences
- Be honest and truthful
- Follow community guidelines
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Respect privacy

### Prohibited Activities

❌ Never:
- Write fake reviews
- Incentivize positive reviews
- Retaliate against negative reviews
- Ask friends/family to write reviews
- Review your own tours
- Threaten tourists over reviews

<Note>
  Violation of review policies can result in review removal, profile suspension, or permanent ban from the platform.
</Note>

### Disputing Reviews

If you believe a review is fake or violates policies:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Document Your Case">
    Gather evidence that the review is fraudulent or violates guidelines.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit Dispute">
    Contact support with details and evidence.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for Review">
    Kin Conecta team investigates (typically 5-7 business days).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Receive Decision">
    If policy violation confirmed, review is removed. Otherwise, it remains.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Review Removal

Reviews may be removed if they:

- Contain false information
- Include discriminatory content
- Are written by someone who didn't attend
- Include personal information
- Violate terms of service
- Are spam or promotional

## Using Reviews in Your Decision-Making

### For Tourists Booking Tours

Evaluate guides using:

**Overall Rating:**
- Look for 4.5+ stars for confidence
- Consider number of reviews (more is better)

**Recent Reviews:**
- Focus on reviews from last 3-6 months
- Older reviews may not reflect current quality

**Review Content:**
- Read detailed reviews, not just ratings
- Look for comments about your priorities
- See if concerns are addressed

**Guide Responses:**
- Do they respond to reviews?
- Are responses professional?
- Do they address criticism constructively?

**Pattern Recognition:**
- Are there consistent themes?
- Do multiple reviewers mention same positives/negatives?
- Any red flags appear repeatedly?

<Tip>
  A guide with 4.8 stars and 50 reviews is generally a safer bet than a guide with 5.0 stars and only 3 reviews.
</Tip>

## Review Statistics and Rankings

### How Reviews Affect Search

Your reviews impact platform visibility:

- Higher ratings rank higher in search
- Number of reviews affects ranking
- Recent reviews weighted more heavily
- Response rate factors into algorithm

### Profile Badges

Excellent reviews can earn:

- **Top Rated** - Consistently high ratings
- **Guest Favorite** - High booking and review volume
- **Quick Responder** - Fast message and review response times

## Review Analytics for Guides

Use your review data to improve:

### Track Trends

- Monitor average rating over time
- Identify seasonal patterns
- Compare ratings across different tours
- Note correlation with changes you make

### Identify Strengths

What do reviews consistently praise?
- Highlight these in tour descriptions
- Emphasize in marketing materials
- Train on these for new tours

### Address Weaknesses

What gets criticized?
- Make improvements immediately
- Communicate changes in responses
- Monitor if feedback changes
